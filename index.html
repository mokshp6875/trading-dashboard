    📖 How to Read This Dashboard
    Win Rate vs Accuracy
    Win Rate = on days the model said BUY, what % did the stock go up. Accuracy = across ALL days (BUY and NO TRADE), how often was the model's directional call correct. Accuracy is more complete.
    Directional Accuracy
    Counts 4 outcomes: BUY+UP (correct), BUY+DOWN (wrong), NO TRADE+DOWN (correct), NO TRADE+UP (missed). Correct ÷ total = accuracy. Above 55% is strong for a daily stock prediction model.
    Profit Factor
    Total gains ÷ total losses on BUY trades. Above 1.0 = profitable. 1.5+ is strong. Matters more than win rate because big wins with small losses can be profitable at 45% win rate.
    Model Confidence
    Score 0–100%. Above 55% = BUY signal. Built from rule-based signals (70%) + machine learning (30%). If ML drops very low it means the model sees a downtrend — it has a floor of 35% to stop it dragging down valid signals.
    Why ML Probability Dropped
    The ML model learned from past data that when the stock is below its moving averages, trades fail more often. So it outputs a low probability. This is correct behaviour — the model is being cautious in a downtrend. It will recover as conditions improve.
    Signal Strength (pips)
    How many of 9 conditions passed. Green = 7–9 (strong). Yellow = 4–6. Red = 1–3. More green pips = higher confidence signal.
    Market Regime
    Bull Trend = best for trades. Sideways = selective. Bear Trend = model mostly sits out. Regime determines how many signals fire each week.
    ⚡ Missed vs ❌ Loss
    Missed = zero money lost. Said NO TRADE, stock went up. You kept capital. Loss = money lost. Said BUY, stock fell. Only losses hurt your account.
    Stock vs SPY
    Both at 0% start. Stock above SPY line = beating the market over that window. Numbers are % change from day 1 — not stock prices.
